To leverage industrial mining with a view to build economic resilience, the Government adopted in 1994 a strategy aimed at developing the mining sector to offset or reduce the dependence on rain-fed agriculture. The implementation of this strategy enabled Malis economy to achieve remarkable resilience. Thanks in part to the sustained growth of the mining sector, Mali has achieved economic resilience in the face of adverse shocks, recurring droughts and major conflict that led to the 2012 economic and political crisis. The government recently adopted a new mining policy aimed at diversifying and integrating mining production into the economy as a factor of sustainable development.
