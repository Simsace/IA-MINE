Malis fragility was exacerbated by the 2012 crisis and its aftermath: For the first time, the territorial integrity of Mali was threatened, raising concerns about Mali's internal capacity to cope with challenges to ensure physical security of its population. The conflict was not an isolated event but the result of pent-up frustrations and unresolved problems dating back to the early days of independence. The crisis demonstrated that one of the main challenges for Mali lies in nation building and political governance. The restoration of people's confidence in public administrations is becoming a priority and the evolution of the way in which Malian democracy operates is now emerging as a major issue. The system of consensus politics has led to the emergence of a procedural democracy with a bureaucracy characterized by elitist, urban-oriented policies and captured institutions which face difficulties to deliver quality services.
